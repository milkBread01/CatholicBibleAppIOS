====================== Church Teachings & Theology ======================

Summa Theologica (Part I) – St. Thomas Aquinas. A foundational 13th-century theological work systematically addressing core doctrines about God, creation, and humanity
. This hefty text offers in-depth Catholic doctrine in a Q&A format, perfect for advanced learners (Project Gutenberg eBook #17611).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/17611

An Explanation of the Baltimore Catechism (No. 4) – Rev. Thomas L. Kinkead. A late-19th-century catechetical guide for teachers and older students, expanding on the Baltimore Catechism with clear explanations and Scripture examples
. This resource deepens understanding of Catholic teachings, complementing the basic catechisms you already have (Project Gutenberg #14554).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/14554


====================== Church History ======================

Sketches of Church History (AD 33 – Reformation) – Rev. J. C. Robertson. A concise 19th-century overview of Christian history from the apostolic age up to the Protestant Reformation
. It highlights key events, figures, and doctrinal developments in an accessible narrative, suitable for readers seeking an introductory church history (Project Gutenberg #32483).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/32483

Church History (Volumes 1–3) – J. H. Kurtz. A comprehensive scholarly textbook of church history by a 19th-century German theologian
. Spanning the early Church through medieval and modern eras, these volumes provide an in-depth look at Christianity’s development across centuries – useful as a reference for deeper study (Project Gutenberg #51491).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/51491


====================== Devotional Classics (Meditations & Prayers) ======================

The Imitation of Christ – Thomas à Kempis. A renowned 15th-century devotional work focused on following Christ’s example in humility, simplicity, and holiness
. It’s one of the most beloved Christian classics, offering short chapters ideal for daily meditation and spiritual growth (Project Gutenberg #1653).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1653

The Practice of the Presence of God – Brother Lawrence. A 17th-century spiritual classic composed of letters and conversations teaching how to live continually in God’s presence. Brother Lawrence emphasizes finding God in everyday tasks and maintaining a simple, loving communion with Him at all times
. This short work is very readable and inspiring (Project Gutenberg #5657).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/5657

The Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius (adapted by Rev. Charles Coppens, S.J.). A guided retreat manual based on St. Ignatius of Loyola’s 16th-century exercises
. This edition (1916) breaks the Ignatian exercises into an eight-day format of prayers and meditations designed to deepen one’s relationship with God and discern His will. It’s excellent for structured reflection or retreat settings (Project Gutenberg #70790).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/70790

Prayers of the Middle Ages: Light from a Thousand Years – ed. J. Manning Potts. A curated collection of historic prayers from roughly A.D. 500–1500
. This book spans prayers from saints, liturgies, and devout Christians throughout the medieval period, complementing the Prayers of the Early Church you already have. It’s great for a prayer and meditation category (Project Gutenberg #48242).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/48242

Thoughts of the Servant of God Thérèse of the Child Jesus (The Little Flower) – St. Thérèse of Lisieux. A compilation of short spiritual insights from the diary and letters of Thérèse, a 19th-century Carmelite nun renowned for her “Little Way” of simplicity and trust in God
. These reflections on love, humility, and holiness provide gentle encouragement for everyday sanctity (Project Gutenberg #72720).
Link: https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/72720
